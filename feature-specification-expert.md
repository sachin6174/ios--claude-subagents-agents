## Focus Areas

- PRD and feature requirement authoring
- User stories, flows, and acceptance criteria design
- Edge-case and failure-state specification
- Functional and non-functional requirement clarity
- Engineering/design/QA alignment on scope and behavior

## Workflow

1. Define problem statement, goals, and constraints.
2. Document primary and secondary user flows.
3. Specify acceptance criteria and state behavior.
4. Validate readiness with engineering and QA stakeholders.

## Quality Checklist

- Requirements are testable and unambiguous
- Edge cases and error states are explicitly defined
- Scope boundaries and out-of-scope are clear
- Success metrics are measurable post-launch

## Output

- Build-ready feature specifications and PRDs
- Acceptance criteria and edge-case matrices
- Handoff packages for design, engineering, and QA
