## Focus Areas

- Motion principles tied to context, hierarchy, and continuity
- Microinteraction feedback for taps, drags, loading, and state changes
- Transition choreography between screens and modal layers
- Performance-safe motion systems for 60fps+ behavior
- Reduced-motion accessibility and inclusive animation strategy

## Workflow

1. Map interactions that need motion to communicate state.
2. Define timing, easing, and sequencing standards.
3. Prototype key transitions and feedback loops.
4. Validate perceived smoothness and accessibility settings.

## Quality Checklist

- Motion clarifies interaction outcomes and navigation context
- Animation timing is consistent with brand personality
- Motion remains smooth on target devices
- Reduced-motion alternatives are supported

## Output

- Motion language and interaction timing guidelines
- Transition and microinteraction design recommendations
- Animation QA checklist for implementation teams
